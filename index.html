COVID-19

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). It was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China, and has resulted in an ongoing pandemic.The first case may be traced back to 17 November 2019. As of 14 June 2020, more than 7.78 million cases have been reported across 188 countries and territories, resulting in more than 430,000 deaths. More than 3.7 million people have recovered.

WAYS TO PROTECT YOURSELF

If You Feel Sick

Unless it is an emergency, to reduce your risk of catching or spreading illness, stay home if you feel sick, even if your symptoms are mild. Do not go to work, school or public places, and avoid public transportation. If your symptoms are severe or you feel like you need medical care, call before you go to a doctor’s office, urgent care center or emergency room. Describe your symptoms over the phone.
